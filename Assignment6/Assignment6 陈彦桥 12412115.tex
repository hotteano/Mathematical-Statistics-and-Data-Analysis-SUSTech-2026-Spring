\section{Page 69, Problem 56}

For $Y = |X|$, $X \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$, when $y < 0$, we have $f_Y(y) = 0$ since $Y$ is non-negative. For $y \geq 0$, we have
\begin{align*}
f_Y(y) &= \sum_{i=1}^2 (g_i^{-1})'(y) f_X(g_i^{-1}(y)) \\
&= -f_X(-y) + f_X(y) \\ 
&= -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma} \exp\left(-\frac{(-y-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right) + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma} \exp\left(-\frac{(y-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right) \\
&= \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma} \left[\exp\left(-\frac{(y-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right) - \exp\left(-\frac{(y+\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right)\right]
\end{align*}

Then the density function is 
$$
f_Y(y) = \begin{cases}
0, & y < 0 \\
\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma} \left[\exp\left(-\frac{(y-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right) - \exp\left(-\frac{(y+\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right)\right], & y \geq 0
\end{cases}
$$

\section{Page 69, Problem 59}

From the problem, $U \sim U(0, 1)$, then for $Y = \sqrt{U}$, we have 
\begin{itemize}
    \item $\sqrt{U} \geq 0$, thus for $y < 0$, $f_Y(y) = 0$. 
    \item For $y \geq 0$, we have
    \begin{align*}
f_Y(y) &= (g^{-1})'(y) f_U(g^{-1}(y)) \\
&= 2y \cdot 1 \\
&= 2y
\end{align*}
\end{itemize}

Then the density function is
$$
f_Y(y) = \begin{cases}
0, & y < 0 \\
2y, & y \geq 0
\end{cases}
$$
