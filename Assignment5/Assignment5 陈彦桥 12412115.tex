\section{Page 67, Problem 33}

We check the following properties: 

\begin{itemize} 
    \item Monotonic Increasing: For any $x < y$, we have $F(x) < F(y)$. 
    \item Right Continuous: $F(x) = 1 - \exp(-\alpha x^{\beta})$ is a continuous function, so it is right continuous.
    \item Limits: $\lim_{x \to -\infty} F(x) = 0$ and $\lim_{x \to +\infty} F(x) = 1$.
    \item Bounded: $F(x) \in [0, 1]$ for any $x \in \mathbb{R}$.
\end{itemize}

Thus, $F(x)$ is a CDF.

The corresponding PDF is $f(x) = F'(x) = \alpha \beta x^{\beta - 1} \exp(-\alpha x^{\beta})$ for $x > 0$ and $f(x) = 0$ for $x \leq 0$.

$$

We calculate the CDF of $X$ as follows:
$$
F(x) = \begin{cases} 
    \int_{-\infty}^x \frac{1}{2}e^{t} \mathrm{d}t, & x < 0 \\
    \int_{-\infty}^0 \frac{1}{2}e^{t} \mathrm{d}t + \int_{0}^x \frac{1}{2}e^{-t} \mathrm{d}t, & x \geq 0
\end{cases}

$$
F(x) = \begin{cases}

\section{Supplementary Problem 2}

We formulate the problem as: $X \sim E(\frac{1}{5})$, and the customer leave if $X > 10$. $Y$ denotes the times that the customer leave, and we have $Y \sim B(5, P(X > 10))$. From the CDF of exponential distribution, we have
$$
P(X > 10) = 1 - P(X \leq 10) = 1 - (1 - e^{-\frac{1}{5} \cdot 10}) = e^{-2}
$$

Thus $Y \sim B(5, e^{-2})$. 

The distribution of $Y$ is
$$
P(Y = k) = \binom{5}{k} (e^{-2})^k (1 - e^{-2})^{5-k}, \quad k = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5
$$

Thus, 
$$
P(Y \geq 1) = 1 - P(Y = 0) = 1 - (1 - e^{-2})^5
$$
